How to become a Frontend Engineer

To become a Frontend Engineer, you need to learn HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, get comfortable with a front-end framework, learn basic design principles, practice building your own projects, and stay up-to-date with industry trends.

Interview questions for Frontend Role

When interviewing for a Frontend Engineer role, you can expect questions about your motivations for pursuing the field, your approach to staying current with the latest trends and technologies, and your knowledge of design principles. It's important to be able to demonstrate your expertise in HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, as well as any frameworks or libraries you're familiar with. Additionally, be prepared to showcase your portfolio and discuss your experience building responsive, accessible, and user-friendly web applications.

Frontend Salaries

Frontend Engineer salaries vary based on factors such as location, experience, and industry. In general, the average salary for a Frontend Engineer in the United States is around $85,000 per year, with entry-level positions starting at around $60,000 and senior positions paying upwards of $120,000. Salaries can also be affected by the specific technologies and frameworks a candidate is skilled in. In addition to base salaries, many Frontend Engineers also receive bonuses, stock options, and other benefits.
